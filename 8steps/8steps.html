Designing for the Web (8 steps)

1)Develop Your Idea

So I want to create a web page that showcases my personal work in a way that expresses my technical skills clearly, but also appeals to potential employers or collaborators. I want the design to showcase creativity while remaining easy to use and intuitive for the user.

2) Discovery and Research

During my exploration, I found some websites that I liked, but they were more artistically experimental and less commercial. However, I also saw a lot of websites for restaurants or brands that were designed to be very clean and interactive, often with a good combination of clean UI design and strong visual presentation. This made me realize that a good design needs to be not only aesthetically pleasing, but also ensure a smooth user experience and clear functionality.
Also, I noticed that many game designers use Itch.io to showcase their game creations. The layout of these pages is very intuitive and attractive, especially to allow users to quickly navigate through each project.

3) Target Your Audience

My audience is primarily technical, design and gaming professionals who may be hiring managers, coworkers, or other design and developers. They should expect to see a portfolio that has both technical depth and design creativity.
