➤ As a Design and Technology student, how do I need to start preparing my web portfolio?

Define Goals: Determine the purpose of your portfolio—showcase your skills, projects, and growth.
Collect Work: Gather samples of your best work, including design projects, technical assignments, and any relevant work experience.
Select a Platform: Choose a platform for your portfolio (e.g., WordPress, Wix, custom HTML/CSS).
Design Layout: Plan a clean, user-friendly design that highlights your work effectively. Ensure it's visually appealing and easy to navigate.
Showcase Projects: Create detailed project pages that describe your work process, challenges, and outcomes. Include images, videos, or interactive elements.
Include a Resume: Add a section for your resume and contact information.
Optimize for Mobile: Ensure your portfolio is responsive and looks good on all devices.
Get Feedback: Ask peers or mentors to review your portfolio and provide feedback.
Update Regularly: Keep your portfolio updated with new projects and skills.

➤ In design and technology major, what do employers look for in a website?

In the Design and Technology field, employers look for a website that effectively showcases your creativity, technical skills, and project experience. They value a professional, visually appealing design that reflects your understanding of design principles and user experience. The website should highlight a diverse range of projects, demonstrating your ability to handle different challenges and tools. Clear, detailed case studies and project descriptions are crucial, as they provide insight into your problem-solving process and outcomes. Employers also appreciate a well-organized portfolio that is easy to navigate, along with a resume and contact information that make it simple to reach you. Additionally, a responsive design that looks good on various devices can be a significant plus, showing that you can create user-friendly experiences across different platforms.

➤ If I only have two years of school work to show, what is the best work to show my skillset and problem-solving abilities?

When you have only two years of school work to showcase, focus on presenting projects that best highlight your skillset and problem-solving abilities. Select work that demonstrates a range of technical and design skills, such as complex design solutions, innovative problem-solving, and effective use of tools and technologies. Include projects that had significant challenges where you employed critical thinking and creative solutions, and provide detailed case studies that explain your approach, the obstacles you faced, and the results achieved. Prioritize quality over quantity by choosing projects that reflect your growth, versatility, and ability to tackle diverse problems, ensuring that each piece clearly illustrates your competencies and accomplishments.

➤ How important is it for my website to stand out and be memorable? What can I do to make it that way?

It's very important for your website to stand out and be memorable, as it helps you leave a lasting impression on potential employers or clients. A unique, well-designed website reflects your personal brand and creativity, which can set you apart from other candidates. To make your website stand out, focus on creating a clean, visually striking design with a clear, intuitive layout that is easy to navigate. Incorporate engaging elements such as interactive features, animations, or unique typography that aligns with your design style. Use a consistent color scheme and branding throughout, and make sure your personality comes through in the way you present your work. Additionally, ensure that your content is concise yet impactful, and that your projects clearly showcase your strengths and problem-solving skills. A memorable user experience can leave a positive impression and increase the chances of recruiters remembering you.
